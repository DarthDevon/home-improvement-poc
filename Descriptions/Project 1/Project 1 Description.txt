Got called over to take a look at a deck that bounced like a trampoline and a spongy feeling floor as you walk inside. 
We noticed the deck joists and the subfloor were rotted. Inside, the only thing from keeping from falling to the basement was the laminate flooring 
As soon as we removed the flooring the subfloor was soft enough we could push a #2 pencil through it. The old door had been rotten for years and had been letting any and all weather through to the wood below
- We cut and replaced the subfloor
- Bathroom had leaking valves so while we had everything apart for the flooring replacement, we re plumbed the area while the home owner painted and the old bathroom got a face lift.  
- Cut out the door
- Added flashing and insulation since there was never any to begin with
- Replaced with a steel French door
- Added PVC trim so help future proof and sealed it around the edges
- We then finished the floor with our supply of LVP and trimmed it up. 
- Tore out the deck and found the bay window had rotted as well so we tore it off and replaced

We got it all trimmed out with a full picture frame finish on the deck surface. 
All white trim with lattice to keep pets and small animals out from under
All aluminum rails
You can see the new French door we installed with the new hardware to top it all off.
