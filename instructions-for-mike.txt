GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE (THIS IS THE BIG ONE FOR LEADS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Real talk: for a local home renovation contractor, your Google
Business Profile (GBP) drives more leads than the website itself.
When someone in O'Fallon types "deck builder near me" into Google,
the first thing they see is a map with 3 businesses pinned on it —
that's the GBP "Local Pack." If you're in it, you get calls. If
you're not, the website matters way less.

Here's the short list of what to do with your GBP, in order of
return-on-time-spent. Each one literally takes minutes.

  1. CLAIM AND VERIFY YOUR PROFILE.
     Go to google.com/business and search for "Howe Renovations
     O'Fallon MO." If a profile already exists, claim it. If not,
     create one. Google will verify by mailing a postcard with a
     code, or sometimes by phone. Do this first.

  2. FILL EVERY FIELD.
     Business name (Howe Renovations), category (Deck Builder + add
     secondary categories: Bathroom Remodeler, Kitchen Remodeler,
     Contractor), service area (O'Fallon, St. Charles, St. Peters,
     Lake St. Louis, Wentzville, Dardenne Prairie, plus whatever
     else you actually serve), phone (636-697-2408), website
     (howerenovations.com once the new site is live), hours, and a
     real description of what you do.

  3. UPLOAD YOUR PHOTOS — A LOT OF THEM.
     Profile photos: your logo, a hero shot of you on a job site,
     and 20–30 of your best Before/During/After photos. Google
     rewards profiles that get photos added weekly. Set yourself a
     reminder to post 1–2 new project photos every week. The same
     ones you already post to Facebook work fine.

  4. ASK FOR REVIEWS — ONE AT A TIME, IN PERSON.
     The single biggest GBP ranking signal is recent, real reviews.
     When you finish a job and the homeowner is happy, ask them to
     leave a Google review. Send the link in a follow-up text the
     same day. Don't try to get 20 at once — Google notices unnatural
     bursts. Aim for 1–2 a month, every month, forever.

  5. POST UPDATES (LIKE A FREE FACEBOOK).
     GBP has a "Posts" feature. Use it the same way you use
     Facebook — share a project photo, a quick story, a "this is what
     we tore out of a wall today." Each post gives you another
     opportunity to show up in search.

  6. ANSWER QUESTIONS / RESPOND TO REVIEWS.
     If someone asks a question on your GBP, answer it within a day.
     If someone leaves a review (good or bad), reply to it. Google
     tracks how responsive you are.

If you do nothing else from this list, do #1, #2, and #4. Those
three alone will move the needle on phone calls within a couple of
months.

The new website helps your GBP too — when your website's contact
info, hours, and service area exactly match your GBP, Google trusts
both more. The new site is being built so it matches whatever you
put on your GBP.
